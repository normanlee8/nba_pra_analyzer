The implemented changes address a significant "Under" bias in the system by correcting structural and mathematical imbalances in both feature engineering and inference logic. By shifting rolling statistics from a median-based calculation to a mean-based calculation in prop_analyzer/features/generator.py, the system now provides the machine learning models with a true mathematical expected value, which is essential for the Poisson and Negative Binomial distributions used to calculate win probabilities. To maintain the model's ability to understand a player's "typical" performance, new standalone median features were introduced for all statistical categories across multiple time windows. In the inference layer, the aggressive line anchoring was loosened—moving from a 65% regression toward the sportsbook line down to 30%—which prevents the system from violently suppressing legitimate "Over" edges discovered by the model. Furthermore, the introduction of symmetric tiering in determine_confidence_tier ensures that volatile "Under" picks are penalized and filtered out just as strictly as "Overs," preventing weak "Under" bets from dominating the final output. These modifications collectively allow for a more balanced distribution of picks by accurately valuing high-upside performances while maintaining a rigorous standard for consistency and confidence across all bet types.
