The recent updates to the NBA player prop analysis system were implemented to address a critical calibration failure where win probabilities were being reported at statistically impossible levels, often exceeding 80% to 90% for standard player props. To resolve this, the system’s feature generation was overhauled in generator.py to eliminate a "double-shift" data lag, ensuring that the model no longer operates on stale information by correctly aligning the most recent game data using time-aware merging. In inference.py, the logic was updated to use np.nan instead of 0.0 for missing features, preventing the model from erroneously projecting a player's output toward zero when data is unavailable. Furthermore, a "Market Consensus Anchor" was introduced to pull extreme projections back toward the sportsbook’s line, recognizing that significant deviations often indicate a lack of real-time context like starting lineup changes. Finally, the probabilistic engine in calculator.py was refined to apply a structural variance floor of approximately 35% to 45% of the projected mean and to treat all cumulative stats with a Negative Binomial distribution, which accurately models the high volatility and skewed nature of NBA performances. These changes solve the "over-confidence" problem by widening the statistical bell curve and anchoring projections to market reality, resulting in win probabilities that stabilize within a realistic range of 52% to 62%.
