The updates to the analyzer implement a comprehensive physical fatigue and geographical travel engine to capture the cumulative physiological toll of an NBA season, which was previously overlooked by standard rest-day metrics. By establishing a coordinate-based lookup system for all thirty NBA arenas, the model now calculates literal flight miles and longitudinal time zone shifts between consecutive games. These additions specifically address "travel weariness"—such as East Coast teams playing late-night games on the West Coast—which historically correlates with reduced three-point shooting efficiency and first-quarter sluggishness. Furthermore, the expansion of the rolling game-count features to include "3-in-4" and "4-in-6" night clusters allows the system to identify schedule density traps where players are likely to face unannounced minute restrictions or "tired legs". By integrating these variables directly into the machine-learning feature set, the model can more accurately predict "flat" performances and provide a more robust handicap for Under props based on biometric context rather than just box score averages.
