The primary objective of these changes was to resolve a mathematical logic flaw in the calculation of Active_Hit% that occurred when evaluating whole-number betting lines, such as 10.0 or 15.0. In the original system, the hit rate was calculated using a simple "greater than or equal to" (>=) check against the benchmark, which caused "Pushes"—instances where a player’s actual performance exactly matched the betting line—to be incorrectly counted as a win for "Over" picks while accurately reflecting a non-win for "Under" picks. By counting ties as successes for only one side of a bet, the system was artificially inflating the success rate of certain plays. To fix this, the code was refactored across the math, feature, and inference layers to explicitly separate historical performance into distinct OVER_RATE (strictly >) and UNDER_RATE (strictly <) vectors. Specifically, calculate_dynamic_hit_rates in calculator.py was updated to generate these separated rates while retaining the legacy >= check for model compatibility. Additionally, generator.py was modified to pass these directional parameters through the feature building process, and inference.py was updated to ensure the system ingests the specific rate corresponding to the recommended pick. These changes ensure that "Pushes" are mathematically ignored by both sides of the calculation, providing a more accurate and honest representation of a player's true win probability.
