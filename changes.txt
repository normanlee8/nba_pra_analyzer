The system has been upgraded to a Segmented SHAP Analysis architecture to address the limitation of global feature importance, which often overlooks the distinct statistical drivers associated with different player roles. By explicitly separating SHAP calculations by player position and prop type, the model can now identify specific "noise" features—such as pace interactions for Centers or offensive rebounding rates for Guards—and dynamically neutralize them during inference by setting them to null. This process forces the tree-based models to utilize their native missing-value logic, ensuring that predictions are based only on the most relevant metrics for a specific bet type. To further enhance operational oversight, the training pipeline now includes high-level duration logging to track the total time required for these advanced calculations.
