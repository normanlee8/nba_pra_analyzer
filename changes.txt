The implemented changes introduce a player-specific residual learning mechanism by calculating rolling Mean Absolute Error (MAE) and Model Bias from historical graded predictions. By integrating PLAYER_HISTORIC_MODEL_BIAS and PLAYER_HISTORIC_MODEL_MAE into the feature engineering pipeline, the system now provides the gradient-boosted models with a direct feedback loop regarding their own past performance on an individual player basis. These changes were made to address instances where the baseline feature set—such as usage proxies or matchup data—consistently failed to capture the unique, erratic playstyles of specific athletes, leading to repetitive projection errors. By feeding these residuals back into the training process, the XGBoost and LightGBM models can implicitly learn to "auto-correct" or discount their standard logic for players who historically defy the model's expectations, thereby narrowing the gap between projections and actual outcomes.
