The recent modifications to the repository introduce a Shot-Location Synergy Engine that enhances the model's predictive accuracy by mapping specific player offensive tendencies against an opponent's structural defensive weaknesses. In scraper.py, the data collection pipeline was expanded to include player shooting distributions from Basketball-Reference, which tracks the frequency of field goal attempts at the rim and from behind the arc. The generator.py file was then updated with logic that cross-references these personal shooting profiles with TeamRankings defensive data, such as "Opponent Points in Paint" and "Opponent Three Pointers Attempted," to generate a normalized SCHEME_SYNERGY_SCORE. These changes solve the previous lack of "Opponent Scheme Synergy" by moving beyond simple teammate adjustments to account for the specific tactical environment of a matchup. By identifying when a player's primary scoring weapon aligns with a defense's high-volume vulnerability, the system can now predict performance spikes based on tactical alignment rather than just raw historical averages. Finally, the definitions.py file was updated to register these new synergy metrics, ensuring the machine learning models can learn and weigh these tactical edges during the training process.
