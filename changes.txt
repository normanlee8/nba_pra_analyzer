The system underwent a comprehensive architectural shift to transition from a mean-based Tweedie regression model to a median-optimized ensemble using Mean Absolute Error (MAE) and Quantile Regression techniques. This change specifically addresses the issue of over-projection by optimizing for the 50th percentile outcome, which aligns more closely with how sportsbooks price player props and is significantly more robust against statistical outliers like high-scoring "ceiling" games. To enhance dynamic responsiveness, the feature engine was restructured to calculate per-minute rates and was directly linked to a standalone minutes-prediction model, ensuring that projections automatically scale based on expected playtime rather than relying on static per-game averages. Furthermore, the market integration logic was evolved from hardcoded "drag" coefficients to a Continuous Bayesian Shrinkage approach, which treats the sportsbook line as a strong prior and blends it with model evidence based on inverse-variance weighting to minimize errors where the model lacks external context. Finally, the training pipeline was enhanced by feeding historical prop lines into the dataset as independent "Market Delta" and "Market Ratio" features, allowing the model to explicitly learn and exploit specific market biases and historical inefficiencies.
