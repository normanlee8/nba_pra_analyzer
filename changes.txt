The recent system overhaul transitioned the modeling architecture from a standard Poisson objective to a Tweedie regression framework to better account for the inherent overdispersion in NBA player statistics, where the variance typically exceeds the mean. To maximize predictive accuracy, all post-hoc manual smoothing and arbitrary heuristics—such as blending projections with seasonal averages or applying hardcoded volatility "passes"—were removed in favor of strict reliance on the machine learning ensemble’s output. These changes resolve previous issues with under-prediction and artificial "safety rails" by utilizing Poisson Deviance for model optimization and dynamically searching for optimal time-decay factors via Optuna, ensuring that the resulting win probabilities are mathematically grounded in the true statistical distribution of the data rather than human-defined thresholds.
