To address the systematic inaccuracies and mathematical biases in the NBA player prop analyzer, several critical structural updates were implemented across the core logic. First, the data leakage in the feature generation process was sealed by enforcing strict chronological merging in generator.py, ensuring that the model can no longer "cheat" by accessing the actual results of a game during its own pre-game prediction. The "double-counting" of minutes was resolved in inference.py by trusting the machine learning model's native ability to handle lineup changes, rather than applying a redundant manual multiplier that was causing projections for bench players to explode. Furthermore, the parlay optimizer was transitioned from a pure probability-based ranking to an Expected Value (EV) model, which prevents the system from unfairly discarding high-value, multi-leg parlays that have mathematically superior returns despite lower absolute hit rates. Finally, the probability distributions for composite props were stabilized by capping historical error multipliers and introducing dynamic volatility thresholds for "Under" bets, allowing the system to correctly identify high-value low-line opportunities that were previously filtered out as too volatile.
