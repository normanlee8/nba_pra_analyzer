The implementation of the FOUL_TROUBLE_VULNERABILITY interaction term directly addresses the non-linear relationship between a player's individual foul risk and an opponent’s ability to draw fouls, a dynamic that independent variables often fail to capture in standard regression or tree-based models. By modifying prop_analyzer/features/generator.py to include a dynamic foul-draw multiplier based on league-median performance, the system now calculates a specific vulnerability score that highlights "nightmare matchups" where the probability of restricted playing time is significantly higher than a season average would suggest. This change solves the issue of linear minute-projection over-fitting by providing an explicit mathematical signal for the model to "slash" projected minutes when a high-risk player faces a high-draw opponent, such as Jaren Jackson Jr. facing the 76ers. These updates were registered across the BASE_FEATURE_COLS and PROP_FEATURE_MAP in prop_analyzer/features/definitions.py, ensuring that every volume-based prop type—including Points, Rebounds, and Assists—is adjusted for the high-impact risk of early benching due to foul trouble.
