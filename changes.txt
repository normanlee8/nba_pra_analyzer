The system was updated to integrate advanced play-by-play (PBP) data from PBPStats.com to shift the analyzer from a basic box-score-reliant model to a context-aware predictive engine. By adding the scrape_pbpstats_base_data function to scraper.py, the system now ingests detailed assist networks, player totals, and lineup-level data. These changes specifically address the "black box" nature of player usage by calculating "With-Or-Without-You" (WOWY) efficiency metrics and "Lost Assist Share" in generator.py, which dynamically adjust a player's projection when teammates are injured. Additionally, the inclusion of foul risk per 100 possessions and "Coach Trust" metrics allows the model to better account for variance in playing time caused by officiating or rotational shifts. By updating training.py and inference.py to recognize these PBP features, the Machine Learning models can now identify non-linear relationships—such as a Point Guard’s assist probability dropping when their primary "shooter" is sidelined—resulting in more accurate, +EV (positive expected value) prop recommendations.
