The architecture of the NBA player prop analyzer was evolved to incorporate a Meta-Labeling layer that shifts the system from relying on hardcoded heuristic thresholds to an adaptive, machine-learning-driven confidence model. By implementing a secondary XGBoost classifier—the Meta-Calibrator—the system now analyzes the specific environmental and mathematical conditions under which the primary regressor historically succeeds or fails against sportsbook lines. This secondary model ingests high-level contextual features such as blowout potential, coefficient of variation (CV), and delta gaps to predict the probability of a "win" for any given pick. This change specifically addresses the limitations of static "Pass" or "Volatile" labels by allowing the system to organically identify "Traps" where the primary model is overconfident or "Hidden Edges" that were previously filtered out by rigid, human-defined rules. Ultimately, this integration creates a self-correcting feedback loop where the meta-model continuously learns from graded historical data to dynamically refine pick Tiers, resulting in a more resilient and statistically grounded betting strategy.
