The system architecture was overhauled to eliminate structural biases and mathematical inconsistencies that previously dampened the model's accuracy. A primary correction involved the "Minutes Disconnect", where predicted minutes are now dynamically scaled into the feature set before projections are made, ensuring that role players stepping into starting positions have their statistical baselines adjusted for increased volume. To improve the reliability of "Unders," the Coefficient of Variation (CV) threshold for low-volume lines was significantly tightened, protecting the system from high-variance "noise" in discrete categories like blocks or steals. The Combo Prop Covariance logic was also updated to derive standard deviation ratios from actual projected stat distributions rather than arbitrary fixed percentages, leading to more accurate probability modeling for PRA and other composite lines. Furthermore, the system now utilizes a positional hierarchy for usage absorption, funneling missing usage from injured players to the most relevant position groups rather than distributing it proportionally across the entire roster. Finally, to prevent the model from over-correcting for anomalies like early ejections, Historical Model Bias features are now winsorized and shifted to ensure the boosting trees remain objective and free from data leakage. These changes collectively ensure that the system's confidence tiers and win probabilities are grounded in rigorous statistical reality rather than linear assumptions.
