The primary updates to the system were consolidated into prop_analyzer/models/inference.py to resolve critical issues with tiering distribution and probability inflation. The previous logic frequently assigned a default of 36 minutes to players when specific minute data was missing, causing the model to hallucinate superstar-level projections for bench players and generate inaccurate 99%+ win probabilities. To solve this, the minute logic was overhauled to use a hierarchy of predicted minutes, last-10-game averages, or season averages; if no reliable data exists, the system now assigns a value of -1.0 and passes on the prop entirely. Additionally, the Coefficient of Variation (CV) calculation was modified to use a safe_denom (the maximum of the line or projection) to prevent volatility metrics from artificially shrinking when projections were wildly overblown. Finally, a new Trap / Info Disconnect filter was implemented to catch projections that deviate from the sportsbook line by more than 35% and 2.5 absolute units, ensuring that "too good to be true" edges are flagged as traps rather than being incorrectly promoted to S-Tier.
