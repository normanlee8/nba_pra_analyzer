The implemented updates introduce a reactive multi-window analytical framework designed to capture immediate shifts in player roles and performance efficiency that are often diluted by standard season-long or five-game averages. By integrating Last 1 (L1) and Last 3 (L3) rolling metrics, the system can now identify "hot streaks" or sudden rotational changes—such as those caused by a teammate's injury or a coaching adjustment—with significantly higher sensitivity. To address the problem of statistical "noise" created by blowouts or inconsistent playing time, the new Last 5 Per-36 (L5_PER36) metrics decouple a player’s raw production from their total minutes, allowing the machine learning models to evaluate true scoring and rebounding efficiency. Furthermore, the addition of the Minute L3 Delta (MIN_L3_DELTA) serves as a leading indicator for role expansion, effectively flagging players whose increasing opportunities have not yet been reflected in their market-assigned prop lines. These modifications, combined with a mathematically safe division implementation using np.divide, resolve previous runtime warnings and ensure that the ensemble models are trained on highly granular, rotationally aware data points.
