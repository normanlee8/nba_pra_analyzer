The primary changes involved a fundamental transition from a Tweedie-based regression objective to a Mean Absolute Error (MAE) optimization framework within the training.py module. Previously, the system erroneously applied a Tweedie distribution with a high variance power to composite statistics like PRA (Points + Rebounds + Assists); while Tweedie is effective for zero-inflated count data, composite stats tend toward a Normal distribution, and forcing a Poisson-style objective resulted in massive projection errors and systematic over-projection. To solve this, the XGBRegressor and LGBMRegressor were updated to use reg:absoluteerror and regression_l1 objectives, respectively, which aligns the model with the median outcome—the exact target used by sportsbooks for setting prop lines. Additionally, the custom PassThroughScaler was replaced with a StandardScaler to ensure the RidgeCV meta-estimator in the stacking ensemble receives properly normalized inputs, preventing uncalibrated base model outputs from skewing the final meta-prediction. These adjustments directly address the previous "S-Tier" overconfidence by ensuring that win probability calculations are based on accurate, median-focused projections rather than mathematically mismatched mean expectations.
