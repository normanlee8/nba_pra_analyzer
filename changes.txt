The updates to prop_analyzer/models/inference.py fundamentally restructure how player prop probabilities are calculated by shifting from a purely theoretical model to one grounded in historical performance data. The primary change involves integrating the system's own historical grading data—specifically the Mean Absolute Error (MAE)—into the standard deviation calculation. By converting the model's past errors into a mathematical variance floor, the system now automatically widens the probability distribution for categories where the machine learning model has historically struggled, such as Points + Rebounds + Assists (PRA). Additionally, the tiering logic was tightened by raising the win probability requirement for "S Tier" status to 69% and introducing a "Trap / High Variance" classification for players with a Coefficient of Variation (CV) exceeding 0.42. These changes directly solve the issue of "overconfidence collapse," where the system previously generated an excessive number of high-tier picks with poor accuracy because it ignored its own projection errors and treated volatile performances as highly certain.
