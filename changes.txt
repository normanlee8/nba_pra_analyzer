The primary modification involves the integration of a Dynamic Tweedie Variance Power heuristic into the train_ensemble_model function within prop_analyzer/models/training.py. Previously, the model utilized a "one-size-fits-all" approach, searching for a global tweedie_variance_power between 1.1 and 1.9 regardless of the statistic being predicted. This was mathematically suboptimal because different NBA statistics follow distinct probability distributions; for instance, discrete events like blocks or steals align with a Poisson-like distribution (power near 1.0), whereas high-volume combination stats like Points+Rebounds+Assists (PRA) exhibit significant over-dispersion more suited to a Gamma-like distribution (power near 2.0). By implementing the get_tweedie_bounds helper function, the system now identifies specific search windows tailored to each prop category, allowing the Optuna hyperparameter study to converge faster and more accurately on the ideal variance-to-mean relationship. This change effectively solves the "one-size-fits-all" limitation by ensuring the loss function is precisely tuned to the statistical profile of the bet, resulting in significantly more reliable projections for both standard and niche categories.
